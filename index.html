Welcome to our Digital Humanities Lab

Digital Humanities is the bridge between traditional humanities and the ever-evolving digital landscape. It involves using digital tools and methods to analyze, interpret, and present human culture, history, and thought. Whether it's digitizing ancient manuscripts, using data visualization to map historical trends, or employing text mining to uncover new insights from literary works, Digital Humanities transforms how we study and engage with human expression.

In our Digital Humanities Lab, Faculty, PhD scholars and M.A. students collectively work on Digital Humanities Projects.

M.A. in Digital Humanities and Social Sciences at the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at the Indian Institute of Technology Dhanbad offers the following courses:

  • Introduction to Digital Humanities (IDH): An overview of the core concepts, tools, and methodologies of Digital Humanities.
  • Statistics: Foundational principles in statistical analysis relevant to humanities research.
  • Text Mining: Techniques for extracting meaningful patterns and trends from large corpora of texts.
  • Corpus Linguistics: The study of language patterns through large, structured sets of texts.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Computational techniques for analyzing and understanding human language.
  • Digital Humanities Projects: Practical, project-based learning experiences that apply digital tools to humanities research.

Students of Digital Humanities hail from a myriad of backgrounds - Sociology, Literature, Engineering, Mass Communication, Biology, and Language Studies) The amalgamation creates a room for interdiscplinary discussion and research in the scope of Digital Humanities.

As part of our Master's program in Digital Humanities and Social Sciences, we are working on a series of collaborative projects.

  • The Trajectory of Computer and Humanities Timeline (M.A. 2026 students)

  • Text Mining Project (As part of IDH and Corpus Linguistics, M.A. 2026 students, upcoming)
